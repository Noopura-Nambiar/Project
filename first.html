Tennis

History

Tennis was made in 12th century France when it was played in the form of ahandball game called 'paume'. The game involved hitting a bacll back and forth by hand. Since then, the game has evolved into one of the largest played games with millions of fans around the world.

Open Era/Grand Slams

The Open Era is the current era of professional tennis. It began in 1968 when Grand Slam tournaments allowed professional players to compete with amateurs, ending the dividion that had persisted since the dawn of the sport. Grand Slams Grand Slams are the biggest tournaments in tennis, with more than 100 players from around the world. There are 4 major slams, the Australiam Open, French Open, Wimbledon, and the US Open.
